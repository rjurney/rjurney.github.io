Japanese Tea Bottom Up: Kukicha, Aracha, Konacha

This month I’m introducing myself to Japanese tea - which is nearly always green. I’ve been introduced to other kinds of tea with expensive grades, and it has me wondering if I really know what to appreciate about ‘fine’ tea.

So this time, with Japanese green tea, I’m learning from the bottom up. I’ve ordered three 'crude’ or cheap teas from Yunomi.us: Kukicha, Konacha and Aracha.

Kukicha or twig tea, also known as bōcha (棒茶), is a Japanese blend made of stems, stalks, and twigs.

Kukicha has a nutty, slightly grassy, intensely umami flavor like no other tea I’ve had. It is said to have less caffeine than other teas, although this varies significantly.

I don’t know much about green tea, but when I read descriptions like “…being composed of parts of the tea plant that are excluded from most other teas,” I start rooting for the underdog.

Kukicha is my favorite green tea so far.

Aracha is processed green tea before it is split and refined into different grades. Called “farmers tea,” aracha isn’t commonly available in stores. It has a wild, complex taste. I only bought 10 grams of Aracha - enough for about 3 cups, the way I brew it.

Aracha is salty. It has a complex taste with a salty ending. It is far gentler than Konacha, but the aftertaste reminds me of it. This Aracha is very interesting, seems to have two natures - the up front taste and the tail. I’m definitely going to buy some more and learn more about it.

Konacha is composed of leftover bits of leaves, buds and stems after tea is processed and separated into different grades. You’ve probably had Konacha before, as it is served in sushi restaurants to erase the aftertaste of raw fish.

Konacha has a thick texture with lots of particles in the bottom of the cup. Its taste is complex, even overpowering, with a taste similar to kelp. It becomes bitter fast when over-brewed, so don’t do that.

Konacha definitely goes well with food. It clears the palette with every sip. It is cheap, too. Fifty grams was $4.51 from Yunomi.us.

Bottom line: Konacha is a tea I’m likely to drink often.

All in all I enjoyed these three teas, and I’m likely to try them again.
