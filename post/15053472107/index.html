Cool Paper - Putting Lipstick on Pig: Enabling Database-style Workﬂow Provenance

@arnabotorg tweeted an interesting paper called ’Putting Lipstick on Pig: Enabling Database-style Workﬂow Provenance.’

What the heck is workflow provenance?

It seems that visual programming (sort of like PigPen) has become popular for scientific workflows:

  • Tavera
  • Kepler
  • VisTrails


And while results are logged, there is no standard way to log how results were arrived at:

“A given workﬂow may be executed multiple times in the context of a single project, generating a large amount of ﬁnal and intermediate data products of interest to the user [9]. When such analyses are carried out by hand or automated using general-purpose scripting languages, the means by which results are produced are typically not recorded automatically, and often not even recorded manually.”

Lipstick uses Pig Latin to provide a SQLish provenance record:

“We present a novel provenance framework that marries database-style and workﬂow-style provenance, by using Pig Latin to expose the functionality of modules, thus capturing internal state and ﬁne-grained dependencies.”

Very cool. Data-workflows are increasingly important in many areas, and Pig Latin is positioned to become the duct-tape for all of them.
